M. Scott Conner's Resume and Project Page

I spent most of 2014-16 working for Google Express in Boston (operated by Adecco) while my wife went to school. I had approached Adecco looking for work just to keep us afloat, but when I got there I found that the organization had a lot to teach me about developing a professional culture, developing a new organization, and the process of developing the tools that it could use to meet its goals. While I was in Boston I also played a few concerts in the Boston Civic Orchestra in the French horn section. I also composed my first ever original music while playing bass in a progressive rock trio. After my wife graduated we spent all of June hiking the 273 miles of the Long Trail in Vermont with our dog Atticus.

During this time I also set my sights on becoming a professional computer programmer. I began to develop spreadsheet based tools at Google Express that required me to think about logical outcomes. I watched my coworkers use these tools and I learned to tweak our tools so that they would be easier to understand, less buggy, and generally more successful. I took advantage of online resources that taught me the foundations of general coding practices, an Java in particular, and I learned about the careers I could pursue with programming skills. I thought that with the requisite coding knowledge and my knowledge of how to be an effective worker, I could be a valuable asset to any organization. And that is my goal. I'm striving to prove that I can be an effective programmer, and that I can provide value to an employer on multiple levels.

This page shows some of the projects I have worked on, and demonstrates some of the skills i have acquired over the last year or so. I have included some personal information as well so you can get to know me a little better, so please feel free to explore the site.
